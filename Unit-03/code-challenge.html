Check-in

Online: Most airlines allow you to check in 24 hours before your flight via their app or website. You'll receive a digital boarding pass.
At the Airport: If you have large luggage to check, go to the airline’s counter. You'll show your ID (and passport for international flights) to get your printed boarding pass and drop off your bags.

Security Screening

This is the "bottleneck" of the process. You’ll put your carry-on bags, jacket, and metal items into bins to go through the X-ray.
Tip: Remember that liquids usually need to be in containers of 100ml or less, and laptops often need to be taken out of your bag and placed in a separate bin.

Locating Your Gate

Once through security, check the large Departures screens. Find your flight number to see which Gate you need to go to.
Airports can be huge, so head toward your gate immediatelyto gauge how much time you have to grab a snack or do some work.

Waiting and Listening

Keep an eye on the clock and listen for announcements. Flights usually begin boarding 30 to 45 minutes before the scheduled departure time.
Your boarding pass will often have a Zone or Group number.
